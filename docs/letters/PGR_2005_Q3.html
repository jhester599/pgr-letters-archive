2005 Q3 10-Q Period ending 2005-09-30

CEO Shareholder Letter

2005 Q3 Letter — Key Points Summary

  1. Catastrophe Response & Disaster Management — Hurricane Katrina generated nearly 30,000 claim reports and an expected loss of $185 million — approximately 5.5 times Progressive's prior single highest catastrophe loss; over 90% of claims resolved by end of October.
  2. Profitability & Underwriting Performance — Combined ratio of 90.4 for Q3 despite Katrina and Rita losses; favorable prior-year reserve development of $93.9 million partially offset catastrophe costs.
  3. Loss Costs & Severity Trends — Favorable frequency continued broadly; $93.9 million of prior-year reserve development recognized as lower-than-estimated severity on closed and open claims; management emphasized accuracy over labeling development as favorable.
  4. Premium Growth — Net premiums written grew 5% for Q3 and 7% year-to-date; year-over-year premium per application down about 5% for Drive and 2% for Direct, reflecting soft market pricing.
  5. Policies in Force & Customer Growth — Personal Lines PIF up 10% year-to-date (Drive auto 6%, Direct auto 12%, Special Lines 14%); Commercial Auto up 12%; Internet-initiated Direct business share increased another 5 percentage points above prior guidance of 50–55%.
  6. Product Innovation & Expansion — Progressive entered New Jersey on September 30 with both Drive and Progressive Direct brands; planned to add Commercial Auto later in 2005 and motorcycle and boat products in 2006.
  7. Claims Service & Innovation — Hurricane response deployed over 1,300 claims representatives; 5,000 Katrina flood-contaminated total-loss vehicles ordered crushed rather than returned to market, reflecting strong ethics commitment.

Few in the insurance industry would describe the third quarter of 2005 with “business as usual” commentary. Katrina, and to a lesser degree Rita, are replacing our memory of the names of the four dominant storms from the third quarter of 2004 and recalibrating the extent of loss possible from such natural disasters. Such is the case for Progressive, but the quarter was also highlighted by our entry into a large state, continued favorable development of loss reserves against our prior expectations and solid, if not robust, growth.

Third quarter shopping activity continued to reflect “soft market conditions” with prices subject to more downward movement than increases. Quarterly growth of 5% on net premiums written and 6% on net premiums earned was slightly slower than the year-to-date rates of 7% for both, signaling some continued slowing. Our year-over-year premium per application was down about 5% for our Drive® branded auto products and 2% for Progressive DirectSM products.

Policies in force is my preferred measure of growth, since it eliminates premium trends. Year-to-date, our Personal Lines policies in force increased 10% (allocated between Drive auto at 6%, Direct auto at 12% and our special lines products at 14%) and were down slightly from the comparable second quarter number of 11%. Similarly, our Commercial Auto increase in policies in force was 12%, down from 13% in the second quarter 2005. While we remain well prepared for additional growth, these results met our expectations relative to the market. Perhaps the most notable commentary on growth continues to be the relative strength of Internet-initiated business for Progressive Direct. In May, we provided ranges for our new customer mix indicating 50% to 55% of our Direct business was Internet initiated versus phone and of those about 60% to 65% completed their sale on the Internet without transferring to a call center. We have now seen these ranges increase by about 5%.

Profitability for the quarter was strong almost belying the included catastrophes. The combined ratio of 90.4 had several moving parts. First, we continued to experience favorable frequency and even some reduced claims activity throughout the country in the week or so following Hurricane Katrina. Second, we incurred the addition of the hurricane losses for both Katrina and Rita. And third, we recognized a continuation of “favorable” development of prior-year reserving. The $93.9 million of favorable development during the quarter reflects the lower than estimated claim severity recognized on closed claims as well as the application of that lower than estimated severity to those claims yet to be resolved.

While industry terminology uses the terms favorable and unfavorable to reflect net contribution to the income statement, we never view significant reserve changes as “favorable” and strive to be accurate at all times. We will continue to monitor both frequency and severity very closely and, if not perfect at prediction, we expect to quickly reflect observations as they develop. We encourage readers of our monthly financials to focus on the accident year loss/LAE ratio as the best indicator of current performance.

Katrina was big in every sense. For Progressive, through the end of October, this translated into nearly 30,000 claim reports, and over 24,000 incurred claims, over 300,000 forms and pieces of mail, over 400,000 telephone calls, deployment of over 1,300 claims representatives and an expected loss of $185 million, approximately 5 ½ times our previous single highest claims event. Our claims organization has been, and continues to be, a source of pride for Progressive and we believe our response to Katrina will further our reputation in both responsiveness and quality of claims resolution. We have every reason to believe that few, if any, companies responded as quickly or as comprehensively as Progressive. As of the end of October, we have resolved over 90% of all Hurricane Katrina claims. As part of the decision making associated with the storm, we concluded that cars significantly exposed to the contaminated flood waters of Orleans and St. Bernard parishes and declared total losses by us should not find their way back into the marketplace. As a result, we have been having these vehicles crushed, shredded and, where possible, recycled. In the end, we expect to crush over 5,000 vehicles.

September 30 was the first day we offered personal auto insurance through Drive Insurance from Progressive and Progressive Direct to New Jersey consumers. On Monday, October 3, Acting Governor Richard J. Codey “officially” welcomed the entry of both brands to the state. Viewed individually, each of our brands is in the top ten of all auto writers in the country. Our path to reentry into New Jersey has been long, and often difficult, but we are very happy to be writing there. Our plans are to introduce our Commercial Auto products later this year and motorcycle and boat products in 2006.

In summary - an eventful quarter with very respectable results. With Wilma highlighting the early fourth quarter, all eyes will continue to be on our response and that of the industry.

Glenn M. Renwick

President and Chief Executive Officer

The Progressive Corporation and Subsidiaries
